What are the environmental consequences of computer device production?

  • Natural Resources
  • E-Waste
 Computer device production can produce lots of electronic waste and deplete our natural resources. Resources that are used in computer device production include silica, iron, aluminum, copper, zinc, lead, selenium, arsenic, tin, nickel, and cadmium. Additionally, computer device production will lead to more computers being thrown away which causes e-waste.

How does computer use harm the environment?

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions
  • Burning Fossil Fuels
Computer use can harm the environment since they use up a lot of electricity. Often, this energy is retrieved from burning fossil fuels which releases greenhouse gas emissions. These gases trap heat in our atmosphere and are one of the causes of climate change, an example would be carbon dioxide.

What are the negative effects of computer device disposal?

  • Pollution
Computer device disposal can harm our environment because of the e-waste that causes pollution. Improper disposal of our computers can also lead to e-waste getting into not just our air but also our soils and water. When this happens, it directly affects us through the air we breathe and sometimes also the food and water we eat and drink.
